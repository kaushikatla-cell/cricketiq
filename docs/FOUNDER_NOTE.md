# Founder note (Common App / portfolio — ~220 words)

I grew up playing cricket with friends in middle school, but by high school our games became irregular and our group slowly drifted apart. The sport was still meaningful to us, yet we had no shared scorebook, no fair way to compare improvement, and no story we could show a coach, a teacher, or a sponsor about what we were building together.

CricketIQ is my answer: a full-stack analytics platform that treats grassroots cricket like a real program. Players get structured profiles, match logs capture batting, bowling, and fielding, and an analytics engine turns raw events into rankings, role recommendations, a Best XI generator, and match win probabilities. Everything runs in the browser first—fast to deploy, easy for a student club to adopt—while still feeling like a serious product you could show to a club, a hackathon judge, or an admissions reader.

Technically, I shipped a Next.js App Router application in JavaScript, with Tailwind UI, Recharts visualizations, localStorage persistence, automated tests for the analytics core, and CI on GitHub Actions. Beyond code, I care about outcomes: fairer selection, clearer development goals, and a stronger community narrative backed by measurable match data.

CricketIQ is not just a coding exercise. It is an attempt to rebuild momentum around a sport I love—using engineering, sports analytics, and product design in service of real people.

— **Kaushik Atla**
